Part I: The Fictional Precursors

The Distributed Mind of Legion

Brandon Sanderson's *Legion* provides a compelling allegory for multi-agent AI. The protagonist, Stephen Leeds, manages a team of hallucinatory experts ("aspects") to solve problems—a direct parallel to an AI orchestrator delegating tasks to specialized agents.

Lem's Cyberiad: Fables of Creation

Stanisław Lem's fables satirically explored the paradoxes of AI. His "Electronic Bard" presaged debates on AI creativity, while other tales highlighted the critical importance of precise instruction—a core challenge in modern prompt engineering.

The Canon of AI

A rich literary tradition has shaped our understanding of AI, exploring themes from rule-based ethics to the nature of consciousness.

I, Robot (Asimov): Rule-based ethics & paradoxes.
Neuromancer (Gibson): Emergent goals & AI agency.
Murderbot (Wells): Free will & autonomy.

Critiques of the Singularity

While the idea of a "superintelligence" is compelling, critics argue the theory oversimplifies intelligence and distracts from present-day harms like algorithmic bias and job displacement.
